The Muir-Óigh of Kilmore Quay

The Muir-Óigh, a figure of Irish mythology, embodies the mystique of the ocean, merging human traits with the enigmatic allure of the sea. Resembling mermaids of lore, the Muir-Óigh are depicted as beautiful women who live beneath the waves, in splendid underwater palaces made from the ocean's treasures.

This sculpture, crafted from the willow trees native to the lands surrounding Kilmore Quay, Co. Wexford, seeks to capture the essence of the Muir-Óigh. It represents their beauty, their connection to the natural world, and the deep, untold stories woven into the fabric of Irish folklore.

The Muir-Óigh are known for their enchanting songs, which could either guide sailors to safety or lure them into peril, depending on the tales told by the sea. Their stories are a testament to the ancient Irish's deep connection with the sea, a vital element of life on an island nation. This sculpture is a tribute to those stories, inviting reflection on the mysteries that lie beneath the waves and the tales that have been passed down through generations.

As you admire this willow sculpture, let your mind wander to the mystical depths of the sea and the legends of the Muir-Óigh that have captivated the hearts of those in Kilmore Quay and beyond.
